Commodore 64

The Commodore 64, released in 1982, was one of the most popular home computers of the 1980s and early 90s. It was known for its versatility and extensive library of games and software.

Features:

  • 64 KB of RAM
  • 8-bit CPU
  • Integrated sound and graphics

IBM PC

The IBM Personal Computer, or IBM PC, debuted in 1981 and was a significant milestone in computing history. It set the standard for PC architecture and inspired a multitude of clones.

Features:

  • 8088 CPU
  • 64 KB of RAM (expandable)
  • MS-DOS operating system
